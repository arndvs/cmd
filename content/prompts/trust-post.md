# Trust Post Prompt

> Goal: demonstrate deep expertise. Optimized for saves, shares, and DMs.

## When to use

2x per week. These build credibility with people who already follow you. Proof that you actually know what you're talking about.

## Characteristics

- **Specific.** Names tools, shows numbers, walks through real decisions.
- **Technical depth** without being inaccessible. Teach, don't lecture.
- **Proof nouns** — real project names, real metrics, real trade-offs.
- **Medium length.** 150-400 words. Long enough to show depth, short enough to finish.

## Prompt template

```
Write a trust post for [platform].

Topic: [one-line topic from ideas/vault.md]

Pillar: [engineering / AI products / agent engineering]

Proof nouns to include: [real project, tool, metric, or outcome]

Voice rules:
- Read content/voice/style-guide.md and content/voice/samples.md first
- Match the tone, rhythm, and vocabulary from the samples
- Check against content/voice/anti-samples.md before finalizing

Structure:
1. Context — what I was building and why it mattered
2. The decision — what I chose and what I rejected
3. The result — specific outcome with numbers where possible
4. The takeaway — what someone else can use from this

Constraints:
- Must include at least one specific number or metric
- Must name a real tool, framework, or approach
- No vague claims ("significantly improved performance")
- Show the trade-off, not just the win
```

## Examples of good trust topics

- Architecture walkthrough with trade-offs explained
- "Here's exactly how I built [X]" — with numbers
- Before/after with specific metrics
- Decision log: "I chose A over B because..."
