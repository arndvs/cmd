# Voice Style Guide

> Explicit rules for how your content should read. The AI checks drafts against these rules.

## Do

- [e.g., Use short sentences. Punch, don't pad.]
- [e.g., Name specific tools, numbers, outcomes — proof nouns over adjectives.]
- [e.g., Open with the insight, not the setup.]
- [e.g., Write like you're texting a smart friend, not presenting to a board.]

## Don't

- [e.g., Don't use "leverage", "synergy", "thought leader", or any word that makes you cringe.]
- [e.g., Don't start posts with "I'm excited to announce..." or "I'm thrilled to share..."]
- [e.g., Don't hedge with "I think maybe" — commit to the take or kill it.]
- [e.g., Don't write a paragraph when a sentence will do.]

## Tone

[Describe your tone in 2-3 sentences. e.g., "Direct, slightly irreverent, technically precise. Casual enough to read fast, specific enough to be useful. No corporate voice."]

## Rhythm

[Describe your sentence rhythm. e.g., "Mix short punchy sentences with one longer one that carries the argument. End sections on the short sentence, not the long one."]

## Platform-specific notes

### LinkedIn
- [e.g., Hook in the first line — it's all anyone sees before "see more"]
- [e.g., Line breaks between every 1-2 sentences]

### Blog / long-form
- [e.g., Headers that work as standalone tweets]
